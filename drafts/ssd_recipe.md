- The SD noise trap
  - Training a model on its own outputs tends to produce very low loss
  - e.g. 0.4; under the perplexity interpretation of "size of likely set" we are at less than two likely tokens at any given step.
  - Quantifying the trap:
    - Proportion and magnitude of changed weights. Initially I was using a fully memory optimized BF16 with no FP32 master weights --> all updates got rounded towards 0.
    - Gradient signal / noise estimator from McCandlish
    - All-checkpoint evals
- Trying to escape the trap
  - Hparams:
    - Intuition: higher LR will not really help --> you just take bigger noise-dominated steps. Found empirically
    - Larger batch size can help, up to a point (according to the estimator, I was typically already above B_crit)
    - Misc results: weight decay, warmup
  - Data
    - No improvement from training on longer or more sequences
    - Some small directional improvement from training on complete sequences only, with the caveat that we then introduce a bias towards correctness.
